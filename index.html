Projects

Senior Capstone

My Senior Capstone Project involved developing a "smart adapter" to control the functionality of "not-so-smart" appliances and devices using basic controls on the adapter itself, and a cross-platform mobile app that works on both iOS and Android. Our team consists of 4 students focused on different aspects of the project. My role was to develop our cross-platform mobile app (including Google Gemini integration) and collaborate with our backend developer to ensure overall system functionality. The mobile app was developed in Flutter using the Dart programming language. The app communicated to our Google Firebase Realtime Database server with user authentication via email using Firebase's in-house APIs. The app interacted with other components of our project using these APIs.

Computational research project

As a Research Assistant in the Thermal Fluid Assisted Medicine (TFAM) Lab at the University of North Texas Department of Biomedical Engineering from October 2023 to December 2024, I contributed to biomedical engineering research focused on computational modeling and medical data analysis under the guidance of Prof. Fateme Esmailie. My work involved analyzing and visualizing complex datasets using MATLAB and Python, automating repetitive computational workflows, and processing CT scan data to generate and extract 3D anatomical models. I also helped conduct fluid dynamics simulations in Ansys to study cardiovascular systems and support ongoing research initiatives. Through this experience, I developed strong skills in scientific computing, technical problem-solving, and interdisciplinary research while working with sensitive biomedical data in a professional laboratory environment.
